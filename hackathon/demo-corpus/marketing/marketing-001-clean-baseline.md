Subject: Notion 2026 launches with native API for the new Workspace SDK

Today we're announcing that Notion 2026 is generally available, with a
new public Workspace SDK that lets developers query, write, and
synchronize Notion content from their own applications.

According to our internal benchmarks, the new SDK reduces median
read-write latency by 38% compared to the legacy v1 API for workspaces
larger than 5,000 pages. We tested this against three production
customer environments — Linear, Cresswell Manufacturing, and the
University of Auckland — over a 30-day pilot in March.

Mark Velasquez, Head of Engineering at Linear, said: "We migrated our
documentation sync workflow in about four hours and our cron-based
backups dropped from twelve minutes to under three. The schema
introspection endpoint was the part we'd been waiting for."

The SDK is open source under the MIT license, with first-class TypeScript,
Python, and Go clients. Documentation is at notion.com/sdk.

Pricing for production use starts at $29 per workspace per month, with
a free tier sufficient for most personal projects. Existing Workspace
Plus customers get production access included.
