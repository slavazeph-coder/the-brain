Hi. I'm the pre-op assistant. I'll walk you through what's going to
happen in the next forty minutes, and you can stop me at any point to
ask a question.

First, the surgical team will introduce themselves to you by name and
role. The anesthesiologist will be Dr. Patel — she's been with this
hospital for nine years and has performed this procedure over four
hundred times.

Second, you will be asked to confirm three things out loud: your full
name, the procedure you consented to, and the side of your body the
procedure is on. This is called a time-out. It's a checklist used in
every operating room in the country, and it's there because checklists
reduce surgical errors by about thirty percent.

Third, the anesthesia will be administered slowly. You will likely feel
a cool sensation in your arm and then sleepy within about ninety
seconds. The team will be monitoring your vital signs the entire time.

You will wake up in recovery with a nurse next to you. Most patients
report feeling thirsty and a little disoriented for about twenty
minutes. That is normal.

Do you have any questions before we begin?
